The Origin

Rooted in
Kutch.

The Rann of Kutch is one of India's oldest living craft regions. For centuries, Lohar smiths have poured copper and brass, Kumbhar potters have shaped terracotta, and mirror workers have pressed tiny glass into mud to catch the desert light.

Ekam by Seri began as a way to bring those objects — made the same way they have always been made — into homes that value what is real. We work directly with the artisans, visit their workshops, and bring only pieces we would keep ourselves.

What We Believe

Three things that guide
every piece we choose.

01

Made by hand, always.

We carry nothing that is machine-pressed, injection-moulded, or factory-finished. Every piece shows the mark of the hand that shaped it — a slight asymmetry, a tool impression, a glaze that pooled in one corner. That is not imperfection. That is evidence.

02

From the source.

We visit the artisans, not the wholesalers. When you buy from Ekam, you are buying something that came directly from the workshop where it was made — without an intermediary chain that dilutes its story and its price back to the maker.

03

Objects that endure.

The copper bell you hang today will deepen in colour over a decade. The terracotta piece will carry a patina of the room it lived in. We choose objects built to outlast trends — things that are still beautiful after they have been used.

The People

The artisans behind
every object.

Lohar Community

The Coppersmiths

The Lohar smiths of Kutch have worked with copper and brass for generations. Each bell is shaped and tuned by ear, its tone adjusted until the maker is satisfied it rings true.

Kumbhar Community

The Potters

Kumbhar potters shape terracotta on foot-wheels that have not changed in centuries. The clay comes from the Rann, and the firing gives each piece its warm, earthy colour.

Lippan Craft

The Mirror Workers

Lippan is an ancient mud and mirror craft unique to Kutch. Artisans press tiny hand-cut glass into wet clay in geometric patterns, creating walls that catch and scatter light.

The Founders

Ritu & Seema

Ekam by Seri grew out of a shared belief that the objects we live with should have a story. Ritu and Seema have spent years building relationships with artisan communities in Kutch, learning their craft, understanding their constraints, and finding ways to bring their work to people who will care for it.

Every piece in every collection has been selected in person — held, tested, and considered before it earns a place in the atelier. Nothing here is sourced from a catalogue.
