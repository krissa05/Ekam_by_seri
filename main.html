From raw earth
to living art.

Each Ekam creation passes through five stages of hand-guided craftsmanship — a slow journey that transforms humble materials into objects of enduring beauty.

i.

Design

Heritage patterns sketched from Kutch textile and architectural motifs.

ii.

Handcraft

Artisan hands shape each form using tools passed through generations.

iii.

Mud Work

Natural mud and rice paste applied in layered relief patterns.

iv.

Mirror Set

Each mirror fragment placed by hand to catch and reflect the warmth of light.

v.

Finish

Final sealing and quality inspection — ensuring heirloom-grade durability.

The Ledger

A house, composed
across generations.

Seven moments in the long quiet of Ekam by Seri.

1947

A craft is gathered.

In the salt-flats of Kutch, the first studio gathers around the women who have shaped mud and mirror through generations.

1968

The pooja chowki tradition is composed.

The lotus motif — drawn from temple thresholds — is brought into the home as a domestic altar form.

1984

A second village joins the atelier.

The mirror tradition of Hodka is invited into the house, expanding the geometric vocabulary of the work.

2001

The atelier rebuilds.

After the Bhuj earthquake, every studio is rebuilt by the artisans themselves, in the old mud-and-mirror method.

2014

Copper joins the house.

The temple-bell families of Nirona are invited to compose the Resonant Series — the first metal work in the house.

2019

The first private commission.

A jharokha is composed for a private residence in Udaipur — eight months in the making, signed by twelve hands.

Today

One hundred and twenty hands.

The house holds 120 artisans across 45 villages of Kutch. Every piece is signed by the maker.

The Living Art of Kutch

Of Kutch.

❦

In the salt-flats and ochre deserts of Kutch, an extraordinary tradition of artistry has flourished for centuries. The women of Kutch perfected the art of mud relief — pressing geometric patterns into clay walls, embedding mirror fragments to capture the desert light, weaving stories of devotion and daily life into every surface they touched.

Ekam by Seri was born from a deep reverence for this living heritage — a belief that these handcrafted traditions deserve not merely preservation, but a place of honour in contemporary homes.
